Website Design in Nigeria: 2026 Pricing Guide

The cost of building a website in Nigeria starts from ₦250,000 and can go up to ₦5,000,000+. This range exists because every business has unique needs. Below is our detailed breakdown of what to expect.

1. Informational Website

Perfect for SMEs and corporate organizations. Its main purpose is to display details about a business to build trust and visibility. These typically have 1-5 pages.

Key Features:

  • check_circle Home & About
  • check_circle Service Listings
  • check_circle Contact Forms
  • check_circle Responsive UI

Pricing: A one-page landing site starts at ₦250,000, while a standard 5-page business site averages ₦750,000.

2. E-commerce Website

A digital marketplace that allows you to sell products 24/7. This system handles everything from product display to secure payment collection.

Key Features:

  • payments Paystack/Flutterwave
  • inventory_2 Inventory Control
  • shopping_cart Shopping Cart
  • local_shipping Order Tracking

Pricing: Professional eCommerce stores in Nigeria start from ₦800,000 to ₦1,200,000+.

3. Custom Web Applications

Built from scratch using modern frameworks (React, Node.js, Python). These are unique systems tailored to specific business logic like school portals or booking engines.

Pricing: Ranges from ₦2,000,000 to ₦15,000,000+ depending on the complexity of the code and logic.

What Drives the Price?

Functionality

Advanced tools like user dashboards, AI chatbots, and complex payment integrations increase development time.

Domain & Hosting

Reliable server space and custom addresses (.com / .ng) are recurring costs ranging from ₦80,000 to ₦120,000/yr.

Content & SEO

Professional copywriting and Google ranking optimization ensure your site actually brings in customers.

Agency Expertise
