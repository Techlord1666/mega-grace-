Cost of Website Design in Nigeria: Pricing Guide 2026

The cost of building a website in Nigeria starts from ₦250,000 and can go up to ₦5,000,000+. This range is quite large because every website differs, and pricing is often based on the scope of the website design project. Knowing what to budget for a website can be tricky because price ranges online are often too wide. In this guide, we will analyze the types of websites, typical cost ranges, and the key factors influencing pricing in 2026.

1. Informational Website

This type of website is perfect for SMEs. Its main purpose is to display information or details about a business or organization. This website usually has no advanced functionalities and often has 1-5 pages. Most companies who need a business website to back their online presence go for this. Similarly, small businesses that need visibility often opt for this website.

Features of an Informational Website: This type of website is built with templates and usually has no advanced features. It does not have payment integrations, inventory management, or booking systems. The sections you would usually find are: Home, About Us, Services, and Contact Us.

Examples:

  • • One-page informational: eds-nigeria.com
  • • Five-page informational: pugantechnologies.com

Cost of an Informational Website: The cost of building a one-page website in Nigeria starts at ₦250,000 while a five-page website costs around ₦750,000.

2. E-commerce Website

An e-commerce website is a website that allows users to buy and sell goods online. It is essentially a digital store. This type of website is suitable for businesses that want to create an online marketplace. E-commerce websites make it possible for businesses to process orders, accept customer payments, and manage product shipping.

Features of an E-commerce Website:

  • Payment Integration: Secure gateways like Flutterwave, Paystack, and PayPal.
  • Inventory Management: Track stock levels automatically.
  • Shopping Cart: Allow users to select multiple items for checkout.

Cost of an E-commerce Website: The cost of an e-commerce website in Nigeria starts at ₦800,000 – ₦1,200,000+.

3. Custom Website

As the name implies, a custom website is custom built for a business or organization. This website is created from scratch using code (React, Node.js, Python, etc.) as opposed to a website design template. Its functionalities are customized to the client’s requirements and so it’s essentially one-of-a-kind.

Why build custom? This type of website is usually built by organizations that want to customize their website offerings to include more than the average experience. For example, schools wishing to integrate online learning into their website may have to build a custom website.

Features of a Custom Website: A custom website usually has a unique UI design and other functionalities as required by the client, such as complex booking systems or custom API gateways.

Cost of a Custom Website: The cost of a custom website in Nigeria often ranges from ₦2,000,000 to ₦15,000,000.

Key Factors Influencing Costs

1. Type of Website

The more complex the website, the higher the cost. Informational is affordable, E-commerce is mid-range, and Custom is the most expensive since it is built from scratch.

2. Features and Functionality

Standard functionality is basic. Adding extras like Payment Integration, User Dashboards, or AI Chatbots takes more time and expertise, which increases the price.

3. Design Complexity

Templates are affordable because the foundational design is done. Custom designs involve creating unique layouts tailored specifically to your brand audience.

4. Domain and Hosting

Domain: Your address (e.g., .com) costs between ₦20,000 - ₦22,000/yr.
Hosting: Costs range from ₦60,000 - ₦90,000/yr depending on provider resources.

5. Developer Expertise

Freelancers are affordable but may lack capacity for long-term support. Agencies provide higher service levels, SEO, and most importantly, peace of mind.

Additional Costs to Consider

Website Maintenance: This ensures your website performs at optimal speed and remains secure. Routine updates include page speed optimization and security patches.

Content Writing: Your website content is the component that tells people what your page is about and guides them to a decision. Content writing in Nigeria typically costs around ₦150,000 - ₦500,000 depending on the volume of pages.

Cost-Saving Tips for Nigeria

  • • Bundle Services: Get hosting and maintenance from the same provider. Most agencies offer packages that save you money compared to buying separately.
  • • Use Templates: Unless you need high complexity, a professional template can save you millions and still look stunning.
  • • Start Small and Scale Up: You can start with a one-page website and scale up as your business grows to manage initial capital.

How to Choose the Right Agency

Check their portfolio to see if their style matches your vision. Ensure they prioritize mobile-friendliness and SEO. Ask about maintenance plans—you don’t want them to build your site and disappear. Remember: Choosing a web agency is like picking a business partner. Take your time, ask questions, and trust your gut.
