paymentsWhy Website Prices Vary So Much in Nigeria

Search "website design price Nigeria" and you'll see numbers anywhere from ₦50,000 to ₦2,000,000. That's not developers pulling figures out of the air — it reflects genuinely different products. A single-page flyer site and a full e-commerce platform with payment processing are not the same job, and pricing them the same way would be dishonest.

The real cost of a professional website in Nigeria depends on a handful of concrete factors: how many pages you need, whether you're selling products online, whether the design is custom or template-based, what happens after launch (hosting, maintenance, SEO), and how fast you need it delivered.

web1. Informational / Business Websites

₦100,000 – ₦250,000

This covers most small businesses, consultants, churches, law firms, and service providers who need a professional online presence — an about page, services, contact details, testimonials, and a way for people to reach you. It's the entry point for getting found on Google and no longer depending only on Instagram or WhatsApp Status.

shopping_cart2. E-Commerce Websites

₦300,000 – ₦700,000

If you're selling physical or digital products, you need a proper e-commerce website with a product catalog, shopping cart, and integrated Naira and dollar payments through Paystack or Flutterwave. This price range also typically includes inventory management so you're not manually updating "sold out" on a DM thread.

apps3. Custom Web Applications

₦800,000+

Booking systems, school portals, membership dashboards, or anything with logins and custom logic falls into custom web development. Cost here scales with complexity — the more the platform needs to "think," the more engineering it takes to build and maintain.

ruleWhat Actually Affects Your Final Price

  • Number of pages and features — a contact form is cheap; a booking calendar is not
  • Custom design vs. template — a design built around your brand costs more than a reused theme, but converts better
  • SEO setup — proper technical SEO so Google and AI search engines can actually recommend you
  • Hosting and maintenance — ongoing costs to keep your site fast, secure, and online
  • Turnaround time — rush delivery usually carries a premium
"The cheapest website is the one that never brings you a customer. Price it against what it earns you, not just what it costs you."

How much does a website cost in Nigeria in 2026?

A simple informational website starts from ₦100,000–₦250,000. A small e-commerce store runs ₦300,000–₦700,000, and a custom web application starts from ₦800,000 depending on complexity.

How long does it take to build a website in Nigeria?

A simple business website usually takes 1–2 weeks. E-commerce stores take 2–4 weeks, and custom web applications can take 4–8 weeks or more.

Is it worth paying for a website in 2026?

Yes — a website is what makes your business findable on Google and AI search tools, and it's the one digital asset you fully own, unlike a social media page.
