Website Design in Nigeria: Pricing Guide 2026

The cost of building a website in Nigeria starts from ₦250,000 and can go up to ₦5,000,000+. This range is quite large because every website differs, and pricing is often based on the scope of the project. If you are looking to build a website then you are in luck because in this guide, we will cover the types of websites and typical costs.

1. Informational Website

This type of website is perfect for SMEs. Its main purpose is to display information or details about a business or organization. This website usually has no advanced functionalities and often has 1-5 pages. Most companies who need a business website to back their online presence go for this. Similarly, small businesses that need visibility often opt for this website.

Features: This type of website is built with templates and usually has no advanced features. It does not have payment integrations, inventory management, or booking systems. The pages usually include Home, About Us, Services, and Contact Us.

Cost: The cost of building a one-page website in Nigeria starts at ₦250,000 while a five-page website costs around ₦750,000.

2. E-commerce Website

An e-commerce website is a digital store that allows users to buy and sell goods online. This type of website is suitable for businesses that want to create an online marketplace. It makes it possible for businesses to process orders, accept customer payments, and manage product shipping.

Advanced Features:

  • • Payment Integration: Paystack, Flutterwave, PayPal
  • • Inventory Management & Stock Tracking
  • • Automated Shopping Cart & Checkout

Cost: The cost of an e-commerce website in Nigeria starts at ₦800,000 – ₦1,200,000+.

3. Custom Website

A custom website is built from scratch for a business or organization. This website is created using manual code as opposed to a template. Its functionalities are customized to the client’s unique requirements. This is usually built by organizations like schools wishing to integrate online learning or businesses needing complex booking systems.

Cost: The cost of a custom website in Nigeria often ranges from ₦2,000,000 to ₦15,000,000 depending on the functionalities required.

Key Pricing Factors in 2026

Design Complexity: Templates are affordable because the foundation is done. Custom designs involve unique layouts tailored specifically to your brand.

Domain & Hosting: Domains cost ₦20,000-₦22,000/yr. Hosting ranges from ₦60,000-₦90,000/yr depending on your site's resource needs.

Content Writing: Professional content guides customers through the buying process. This adds ₦150,000-₦500,000 to your budget.

Maintenance: Ensuring speed, security updates, and routine health checks is vital for long-term digital success.
